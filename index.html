Meaningful Data - Chapter 6

In Chapter 6, Cairo talks about how interesting stories can arise from both norm and exceptions. Cairo doesn't explicitly define what is counted as a norm, but from my understanding, it is something like basic statistical measure, like the mode, median,the mean, standard deviation, etc.. Those are sounded tedious, but in fact quite useful in terms of providing a foudation for us to analyze the data. Those foundations can help us tell a better story

Once we have the foundation, exceptions help make the story more attracting. The exceptions are something exclusive to the norm, such as outlier, or just something different. The example in the book is about how Michael Jordan affected the starting salaries of people graduating from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. His salaries drew the number to an unexpectly high level. This is a story that we cannot tell if simply just looking at the norm. Although Cairo doesn't define the exception, I believe it is something extra to help us understand the data.
